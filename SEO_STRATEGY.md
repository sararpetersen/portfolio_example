# SEO strategy for sararingkow.dk

## Goal and audience

The site should attract organisations, agencies and hiring managers looking for a designer who combines accessible UX/UI with front-end implementation. Social media should support discovery, while search traffic should land on focused case studies and useful articles.

Search volume and competition must be validated in Google Search Console, Google Keyword Planner or another SEO tool. The phrases below are relevance-led opportunities, not unverified volume claims.

## Content ideas

1. **How to design websites for autistic users: a practical UX checklist**  
   Target: `how to design websites for autistic users`. Include examples, mistakes to avoid and links to relevant portfolio features.
2. **Neurodivergent UX design principles beyond WCAG compliance**  
   Target: `neurodivergent UX design principles`. Connect lived experience with concrete interface decisions.
3. **Accessible website redesign: before-and-after case study**  
   Target: `accessible website redesign case study`. Show the problem, audit, decisions, implementation and measurable outcome.
4. **Accessible dark mode: contrast, motion and user preferences**  
   Expand the existing post around `accessible dark mode best practices` and add internal links to the portfolio implementation.
5. **How I test an accessible website before launch**  
   Target: `website accessibility testing checklist`. Cover keyboard, screen reader, zoom, contrast and reduced motion.
6. **Designing clear interfaces for ADHD and cognitive accessibility**  
   Target: `UX design for ADHD users`. Use practical layout and content examples.

## On-page rules

- Give each page one distinct search intent and one descriptive H1.
- Use the primary phrase naturally in the title, H1, introduction and one subheading; avoid repetition for its own sake.
- Keep titles roughly within 50–60 characters when possible and descriptions around 140–160 characters.
- Give every case study indexable text covering the challenge, role, process, accessibility decisions and outcome.
- Link every new article to one relevant case and to the contact page; link the case back to the article.
- Use descriptive link text instead of “click here”.
- Add meaningful image alt text; decorative images should have empty alt text.

## Distribution and measurement

- Connect Google Search Console and submit `https://sararingkow.dk/sitemap-index.xml`.
- Record a baseline for organic clicks, impressions, click-through rate and enquiries.
- Review Search Console queries monthly. Improve pages with high impressions but low click-through rates first.
- Turn each article into one LinkedIn post and several Pinterest assets, all linking to the article rather than only to the homepage.
- Add campaign tags to social links, for example `?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=accessibility_article`, so social traffic can be separated from organic search in analytics.
- Evaluate after 8–12 weeks; SEO gains are cumulative and should not be judged from a few days of data.
